Jalan Tunjungan is a popular destination for both locals and tourists, as it is home to several high-end shopping malls, such as Tunjungan Plaza, Surabaya Plaza, and Grand City Mall. These malls offer a wide range of retail stores, restaurants, cafes, and entertainment options. In addition to its shopping centers, Jalan Tunjungan is also known for its historical landmarks, such as the Gedung Negara (State Building) and the Tugu Pahlawan (Heroes Monument). The street is also home to several hotels, office buildings, and government institutions. Overall, Jalan Tunjungan is considered one of the most vibrant and bustling streets in Surabaya, offering a unique blend of modernity and tradition.

Alun-alun Surabaya is a large public square located in the heart of Surabaya, the second-largest city in Indonesia. It is a popular gathering place for locals and visitors alike, and is known for its historic landmarks, cultural significance, and vibrant atmosphere. The square is surrounded by several important buildings and institutions, such as the City Hall, the Governor's Office, and the Bank of Indonesia. It is also home to several historical monuments, such as the Heroes Monument and the Surabaya Submarine Monument, which commemorate the city's role in Indonesia's struggle for independence. Alun-alun Surabaya is a popular destination for people of all ages, who come to enjoy the street vendors, musicians, and other performers who regularly entertain visitors. The square is also known for its unique cultural events, such as the annual Bungkarno Festival, which celebrates the legacy of Indonesia's first president, Sukarno. Overall, Alun-alun Surabaya is a vibrant and lively public space that offers a window into the rich culture and history of Surabaya and Indonesia as a whole.
