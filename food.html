Food

Rujak Cingur is a salad-like dish made with a variety of sliced fruits and vegetables, such as cucumber, pineapple, and mango, and served with boiled beef snout or cingur. The word "cingur" refers to the snout of the cow, which is boiled until tender and sliced into small pieces. The snout is then mixed with the fruits and vegetables and dressed with a spicy sauce made from shrimp paste, chili, and tamarind. Rujak cingur is a popular dish in East Java and is often served as a snack or a light meal. It is known for its unique combination of sweet, sour, and spicy flavors, and is considered a must-try dish for those visiting Surabaya.

Lontong Balap is is a savory snack made from lontong (compressed rice cake) that is served with bean sprouts, tofu, and lentho (a type of deep-fried fritter made from mashed mung beans). The dish is usually eaten as a light meal or a snack, and it is commonly sold by street vendors in Surabaya. The name "balap" in lontong balap refers to the fact that the dish is usually served quickly to customers who are in a hurry, as if in a race. The dish is usually served with a spicy peanut sauce, sweet soy sauce, and chili sauce, which gives it a flavorful and spicy taste. It is a popular dish in East Java and can be found in many traditional markets and street food stalls throughout the province.
